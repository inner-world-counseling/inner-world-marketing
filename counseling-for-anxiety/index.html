High-Functioning Anxiety Therapy for Professionals

Understanding Your Anxiety

You may have been told that your anxiety is just a chemical imbalance or a series of "worry thoughts" that you need to think your way out of. If you have tried breathing exercises, positive thinking, or traditional talk therapy and still feel stuck, it is likely because your anxiety is not just a mental habit.

For many of my clients, anxiety is a biological alarm system that never learned how to turn off.

Whether you are dealing with a formal diagnosis of generalized anxiety disorder or the chronic, low level dread that often accompanies complex trauma , you are likely exhausted from living in a state of high alert. I help you move beyond surface level coping strategies to address the root causes of that internal tension.

When Anxiety Looks Like Success

If you are a high-achiever who is always "on," an over-thinker, or a perfectionist, your constant worry may look like success to everyone else. However, simply "managing" symptoms isn't the same as "healing" the root cause. We use evidence-based approaches like EMDR to help address the underlying reasons for your perfectionism and burnout, rather than just treating the surface-level symptoms.

What We Will Address Together

When anxiety is rooted in your history, it often shows up in ways that feel impossible to control. We will focus on:

  • The Biology of Safety: Understanding how your nervous system developed its high alert settings as a way to keep you protected during your formative years.
  • Chronic Worry as a Shield: Exploring how your brain uses worry as a way to predict danger and maintain control over unpredictable situations.
  • The Body Connection: Addressing the physical symptoms of anxiety, such as sleep disruption, muscle tension, and the need for external coping tools to quiet your brain.
  • Healing the Roots: Moving past symptom management to address the underlying trauma that fuels your anxiety, allowing you to settle into a more natural state of calm.

An Evidence Based Path Forward

I do not believe in cookie cutter treatment. Because your brain is unique, we will use a collaborative approach to tailor your care. I lean on proven methods like EMDR and CPT to help you process the past. This work allows your nervous system to learn that the dangers of your childhood are no longer present, which finally provides the space for your anxiety to fade.

You do not have to live in a constant state of urgency. If you are ready to stop managing your anxiety and start healing the patterns that sustain it, I am here to help.

Addressing GAD and Daily Worry

While many people struggle with the underlying trauma that fuels anxiety, some also live with Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD). This often presents as a relentless "what if" cycle—a brain that is constantly scanning the future for danger. If you feel like your mind is running a marathon while you are trying to sleep, you are not just "a worrier"; you are managing a physiological state of alarm.

Practical Tools for Immediate Relief

I do not believe in waiting months to see progress. While we work on healing the roots of your anxiety, I also provide practical, evidence based tools to help you reclaim your daily life:

  • CBT (Cognitive Behavioral Therapy): We will identify the specific patterns and "what if" loops that keep your anxiety high. By breaking these down into smaller, manageable parts, we can disrupt the cycle before it dictates your day.
  • Solution Focused Strategies: We will identify the small, concrete changes you can make this week to build momentum. We focus on what is currently working for you and amplify those strengths, helping you regain a sense of agency and control.
  • Nervous System Regulation: Anxiety is not just a mental exercise. We will use targeted techniques to down regulate your nervous system, teaching your body how to shift from "fight or flight" into a state of rest.

My goal is to provide you with a toolkit that you can use the moment you feel the tension rising. You will walk away from our sessions with more than just a place to vent; you will have a clear, actionable plan to manage your symptoms while we do the deeper work of healing the past.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is high-functioning anxiety?

High-functioning anxiety is when you experience intense anxiety, overthinking, and worry, but you still manage to excel in your career and daily life. It often presents as perfectionism, the inability to relax, and being always "on," masking the internal struggle.

Will therapy make me lose my drive or motivation?

No. Healing from anxiety doesn't mean losing your edge. Therapy helps you transition from being driven by fear and panic to being driven by purpose and passion, allowing you to achieve success without the cost of burnout and exhaustion.

How does EMDR help with physical symptoms of anxiety?

EMDR helps process the underlying trauma and stress that keep your nervous system in a constant state of "fight or flight." By addressing these root causes, EMDR can significantly reduce physical symptoms like muscle tension, racing heart, and sleep disruption.

EMDR:
An evidence-based psychotherapy that enables people to heal from the symptoms and emotional distress that are the result of disturbing life experiences.
CPT:
A specific type of cognitive behavioral therapy that helps patients learn how to modify and challenge unhelpful beliefs related to trauma.
